CS 1.6

Counter-Strike (also known as Half-Life: Counter-Strike or Counter-Strike 1.6) is a tactical first-person shooter game developed by Valve.

It was initially developed and released as a Half-Life modification by Minh "Gooseman" Le and Jess Cliffe in 1999, before Le and Cliffe were hired and the game's intellectual property acquired.

Counter-Strike was released by Valve for Microsoft Windows in 2000, and is the first installment in the Counter-Strike series. Several remakes and ports were released on Xbox, as well as OS X and Linux.

CS SOURCE

Counter-Strike: Source is a tactical first-person shooter video game developed by Valve and Turtle Rock Studios.

As in the original, Counter-Strike: Source pits a team of counter-terrorists against a team of terrorists in a series of rounds.

The game was initially bundled with all retail and digital copies of Half-Life 2, before being released standalone.

CS:GO

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) is a 2012 multiplayer tactical first-person shooter developed by Valve and Hidden Path Entertainment.

Developed for over two years, Global Offensive was released for OS X, PlayStation 3, Windows, and Xbox 360 in August 2012, and for Linux in 2014.

Valve still regularly updates the game, both with smaller balancing patches and larger content additions.
